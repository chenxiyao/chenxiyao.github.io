Conflict

Today, let's think about conflict. And let's talk about the problems.
1. Discuss a time you had a conflict. What was it about? What happened?
2. What was the source of the conflict?
3. What basic emotions did you feel upon analyzing the conflict?
4. What actions did you take to resolve your conflict?
5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?
6. What did you learn from this experience?

I remember when I was freshman and lived in the dorm. My roommate nearly created a party once a week, and the party will last very late - even the morning of the next day - on Saturday or Sunday. I felt upset and angry because I cannot study the whole night and even cannot sleep well. I beared several weeks because I didn't want to trouble others. I understand it's the life of her.

However, after several weeks, I found it really hurt my life. And I felt really angry because I respected her lifestyle, but she didn't respect mine. During this week, I let her know I feel uncomfortable because of her so long party.

Then, I talked with her what I am feeling and how her long party troubled me. I also gave her some suggestions like having the party in some friends' home who live alone. Or finish the party earlier at least before 11:00pm.

After that, she understood clearly and she reduce the times of the party and finish them earlier. From this experience, I found that when we meet conflict, we need to try to solve it. It's not really hard like what we think at the first!
